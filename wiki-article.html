Grace Hopper

Biography

Grace Hopper (1906–1992) was a pioneering American computer scientist, U.S. Navy rear admiral, and mathematician famous for developing the first compiler and leading the creation of COBOL, one of the earliest high-level programming languages, as well as for her work on the Harvard Mark I computer.

Recent News

The Grace Hopper Celebration (GHC) is a major annual tech conference named in her honor—it’s the world’s largest gathering of women and non-binary technologists. This year (2025), the Grace Hopper Celebration will be held in Chicago, Illinois, at McCormick Place from November 4–7, 2025
